Goal

The goal of this seminar is to study flat connections in positive and mixed characteristic. One of the main goals is to understand spectacular recent work of Esnault-Gröchenig. To this end, we will survey the theory of p-curvature, mod p nonabelian Hodge theory, the Hitchin fibration, and (iso)crystals. Time/enthusiasm permitting, we may discuss applications to characteristic 0 algebraic geometry using these techniques, in particular the work of Arapura and Langer. A preliminary program may be found here.

References

The following are orienting references. For a complete list of references (with full bibliographic information), please see the seminar program.
  • [EG20], Esnault, H.; Gröchenig, M. Rigid connections and F-isocrystals.
  • [E22], Esnault, H. Lectures on local systems in algebraic-arithmetic geometry
  • [G15], Gröchenig, M. Moduli of flat connections in positive characteristic
  • [K16], Kedlaya, K. Notes on isocrystals. If the crash course on crystals/isocrystals was too succinct, Kedlaya's article is a wonderful resource.
  • [LSZ15], Lan, G.; Sheng, M.; Zuo, K. Nonabelian Hodge theory in positive characterstic via exponential twisting
  • [LSZ21], Lan, G.; Sheng, M.; Zuo, K. Semistable Higgs bundles, periodic Higgs bundles and representations of algebraic fundamental groups
  • [OV07], Ogus, A.; Vologodsky, V. Nonabelian Hodge theory in characteristic p
  • [Oss], Osserman, B. Connections, curvature, and p-curvature. These are notes of Brian Osserman that I found helpful/clarifying, especially for Mochizuki's perspective on p-curvature.
  • [Yun19] Hao, Y. A Simpson Correspondence for Abelian Varieties in Positive Characteristic. This is the PhD thesis of Y. Hao, which is a very helpful guide to Gröchenig's perspective on the BNR correspondence.
